Comparison of Symmetric Encryption Algorithms by Security Margin

Algorithm Key Size Block Size Rounds Notes
Threefish-1024 1024 bits 1024 bits 80 Largest key & block, highest security margin; slower, less ecosystem support.
Threefish-512 512 bits 512 bits 72 High margin, better performance than 1024; moderate ecosystem support.
Serpent-256 256 bits 128 bits 32 AES finalist, heavily analyzed; slower in software, bit-slice friendly.
AES-256-GCM 256 bits 128 bits 14 Hardware-accelerated (AES-NI, ARM Crypto), ubiquitous, blazing fast.
ChaCha20-Poly1305 256 bits Stream cipher 20 Very fast in software, well-studied, widely used in TLS.

Recommendation

  • Threefish-1024: Maximum theoretical margin, but slower and less supported.
  • Serpent-256 or Threefish-512: Overkill yet moderately practical.
  • AES-256-GCM or ChaCha20-Poly1305: Best balance of performance, support, and security.
